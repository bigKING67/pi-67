# Content, Creative, and Social Marketing

## 1. Content system

Define a reusable system rather than isolated posts:

- Audience and journey stage.
- Content mission and platform job.
- Content pillars and proof sources.
- Formats, creator roles, cadence, search terms, and conversion destination.
- Asset ownership, approval, reuse, paid-amplification rights, and refresh rules.

Balance five content jobs: attention, education, proof, conversion, and retention. A healthy program does not make every asset perform all five jobs.

## 2. Xiaohongshu

Use Xiaohongshu for trust, scenario discovery, comparison, search mindshare, and durable user-language assets.

Build:

- Audience x scenario x problem x evidence map.
- Brand, category, scenario, question, comparison, and competitor keyword map.
- Creator tiers: authority, vertical, lifestyle, experience, KOC, and long-tail search.
- Note formats: personal experience, routine, tutorial, comparison, list, myth correction, and use-case diary.
- Comment and search handoff to owned channels or commerce landing.

Avoid identical scripts, unsupported claims, false experience, artificial comments, and volume without search or conversion learning.

## 3. Douyin organic content

Define hook, tension, proof, product role, next action, and landing destination.

Use distinct content families:

- Problem and symptom recognition.
- Scenario demonstration.
- Product or parameter proof.
- Expert or authority explanation.
- Lifestyle and identity.
- Comparison and objection handling.
- Creator story.
- Live-room or search handoff.

Track hook retention, completion, engagement quality, search lift, product clicks, qualified traffic, asset reuse, and refund-safe conversion. Do not optimize only for raw views.

## 4. Creator system

Assign creators by job rather than follower tier alone:

- Authority and trust.
- Cultural relevance and brand meaning.
- Scenario penetration.
- Product education.
- Search asset creation.
- Conversion and live sales.
- KOC proof and long-tail coverage.

The brief should define audience, insight, message, non-negotiable proof, claim boundary, creative freedom, deliverables, usage rights, paid amplification, approval, timing, measurement, and learning capture.

## 5. Creative platform

Translate strategy into a scalable creative system:

1. Single-minded proposition.
2. Consumer tension.
3. Creative idea or organizing metaphor.
4. Message hierarchy.
5. Visual and verbal codes.
6. Hero asset and modular derivatives.
7. Platform adaptations.
8. Testable variables.

Keep brand constants stable while testing hooks, formats, creators, proof, offers, pacing, and CTA.

## 6. Asset specifications

For each asset define objective, audience, format, duration or dimensions, message, proof, CTA, source files, naming, owner, approval, usage rights, channels, expiry, variants, metrics, and refresh trigger.

Use actual design/image skills for visual production. This reference owns the marketing brief and quality criteria, not generic UI implementation.

## 7. Creative QA

Check:

- Brand recognition and message clarity.
- Audience and scenario relevance.
- Proof, claim, and compliance integrity.
- Platform-native readability and pacing.
- Conversion handoff and price consistency.
- Rights, source, and expiry metadata.
- Test isolation: change one decisive variable when learning matters.
- Accessibility and legibility where applicable.
