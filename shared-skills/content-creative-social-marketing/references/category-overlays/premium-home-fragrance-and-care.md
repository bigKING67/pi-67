# Premium Home Fragrance and Care Overlay

## Use when

Use for home fragrance, candles, diffusers, laundry/home scent, premium cleaning, home-care rituals, or giftable home-lifestyle products.

## Demand and trust

- Demand is shaped by atmosphere, identity, ritual, aesthetics, gifting, functional cleaning, and perceived safety in the home.
- Scent is difficult to communicate remotely; trust depends on descriptive accuracy, sampling, comparison, ingredient/material clarity, and credible use guidance.

## Product and evidence

- Record scent family/notes, intensity, room/use case, duration, material, ingredients, surface compatibility, ventilation, storage, and safety instructions.
- Separate sensory description, measured performance, material safety, cleaning efficacy, and subjective preference.

## Commercial risks

- Sampling, glass/bottle breakage, leakage, shipping weight, gift packaging, seasonal sets, and preference refunds can materially affect contribution.
- Avoid over-expanding scent variants before hero profiles and replenishment are proven.

## Operations and supply risks

- Monitor fragrance/material consistency, leakage, wick/burn behavior, packaging, temperature, breakage, surface damage, and complaint traceability.
- Service must distinguish preference mismatch, misuse, product defect, shipping damage, and safety incident.

## Compliance boundaries

- Avoid unsupported non-toxic, antibacterial, therapeutic, allergy-safe, pet-safe, child-safe, or universal-surface claims.
- Fire, ventilation, ingestion, contact, storage, and surface-use warnings must be explicit where relevant.

## Lifecycle

- Model sampling, first full-size purchase, seasonal rotation, replenishment, collection behavior, gifting, and household multi-room use.

## Specialist application

Brand and Content own sensory language and ritual; Commercial owns sample/breakage economics; Operations owns packaging and safety service; Analytics separates preference from defect.
