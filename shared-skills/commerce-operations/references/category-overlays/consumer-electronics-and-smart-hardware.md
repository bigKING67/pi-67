# Consumer Electronics and Smart Hardware Overlay

## Use when

Use for consumer electronics, smart hardware, wearables, connected devices, small appliances, or specification- and service-heavy products.

## Demand and trust

- Demand is driven by use-case improvement, performance, compatibility, convenience, design, ecosystem, reliability, and service confidence.
- Trust depends on clear parameters, demonstrations, independent proof, reviews, warranty, software support, privacy, and after-sale capability.

## Product and evidence

- Record model, specification, compatibility, connectivity, power, environment limits, software/app requirements, accessories, installation, warranty, and update policy.
- Demonstrations must state conditions; benchmark and comparison claims require reproducible scope.

## Commercial risks

- Warranty, returns, installation, accessories, support, replacement, channel price comparison, and model obsolescence materially affect contribution.
- One hero model creates concentration risk; too many models create inventory and support complexity.

## Operations and supply risks

- Monitor component/firmware versions, batch quality, serial/lot traceability, spare parts, packaging, delivery, installation, activation, connectivity, warranty, and repair turnaround.
- Separate setup error, compatibility, software, hardware defect, expectation mismatch, and logistics damage.

## Compliance boundaries

- Safety, radio, electrical, battery, privacy, performance, energy, health, waterproof, and durability claims require applicable certification and test conditions.

## Lifecycle

- Model launch, activation, accessory attachment, software engagement, consumables, warranty, upgrades, replacement, and end-of-support.

## Specialist application

Content owns parameter translation; Commercial owns warranty/service economics; Operations owns setup and after-sale; Analytics owns activation, defects, returns, cohorts, and support evidence.
