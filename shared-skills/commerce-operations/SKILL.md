# Commerce Operations

## Role

Translate commercial and marketing decisions into reliable channel execution. Own daily operating rhythm, service quality, and consumer-facing fulfillment.

## Workflow

1. Identify platform, operating object, owner, cadence, and target.
2. Confirm assortment, price, campaign, and budget constraints.
3. Map the consumer path from traffic to store/live/shelf to payment and after-sale.
4. Define SOP, checkpoints, exception handling, and escalation.
5. Confirm inventory, gift, fulfillment, service, refund, and compliance readiness.
6. Define daily, weekly, and monthly operating reviews.

## Owned capabilities

- Store, shelf, product-detail, search landing, reviews, Q&A, and buyer-show operations.
- Brand self-live and talent-live execution, product sequence, script operations, and room dashboards.
- Campaign calendar, product setup, benefits, gifts, stock coordination, and launch checklists.
- Customer service, after-sale, refunds, complaints, and platform compliance execution.
- Consumer-facing fulfillment and operational exception recovery.
- Owner/action/metric/cadence operating plans.

## Boundary

Commercial Strategy owns prices and economics. Marketing owns audience, communication, content strategy, and paid-media strategy. Analytics owns data definitions. Future Supply Chain owns demand planning, procurement, production, upstream inventory, and warehouse-network capability.

## Output contract

Output objective, prerequisites, SOP, owner, cadence, operating dashboard, exception path, stop condition, recovery action, and next review.
