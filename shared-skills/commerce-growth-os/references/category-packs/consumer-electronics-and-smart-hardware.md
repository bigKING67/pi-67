# Consumer Electronics and Smart Hardware Category Pack

## Load when

Use this pack for 3C/digital products, smart home devices, small appliances, consumer electronics, audio/camera/wearable products, personal-care devices, and parameter/service-heavy smart hardware. For medical devices or regulated health hardware, require current legal/platform verification before claims.

## Category operating logic

Consumer electronics and smart hardware sell parameter clarity, scenario proof, service certainty, and trust:

- Parameters before hype: model, spec, compatibility, performance, warranty, and service promise must be clear.
- Demo before scale: users need to see the product solve a real scenario, not just hear a claim.
- Review/Q&A before paid growth: parameter-heavy categories convert through questions, comparisons, reviews, and after-sale confidence.
- Service before GMV: installation, repair, warranty, return, accessories, and firmware/app support can determine profit quality.
- Price-line separation before channel expansion: Tmall/JD/Douyin live/Pinduoduo comparisons can quickly expose inconsistent pricing.

## SKU matrix

| SKU role | Electronics/smart-hardware example | Job | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Entry model | Basic version, older generation, lower memory/spec | Lower trial barrier | Can anchor brand too low |
| Hero model | Main device or flagship set | Brand image and GMV | Direct price comparison across channels |
| Pro model | Higher spec, larger capacity, premium material | Margin and upgrade | Needs clear proof of incremental value |
| Bundle SKU | Device + accessory + consumable/refill | Raise AOV and reduce comparison | Bundle value must be easy to understand |
| Accessory/refill | Filter, blade, brush head, case, battery, consumable | Repurchase and service ecosystem | Low-value SKU can be logistics-inefficient |
| Service SKU | Extended warranty, installation, maintenance, setup | Trust and conversion | Requires real service capacity |
| Clearance model | Old generation, open-box/isolated SKU if allowed | Inventory digestion | Price memory and service burden |

## Price and margin strategy

Define:

- Official price by model/spec.
- Daily coupon price.
- Campaign price.
- Live-room bundle price.
- JD service/warranty value.
- Tmall flagship/member price.
- Channel-exclusive bundle/spec.
- Minimum profit price and forbidden floor price.

Prefer spec, warranty, bundle, accessory, installation, or service differentiation before visible price cuts. For high-return or service-heavy devices, calculate net profit with return freight, repair/replacement, warranty reserve, customer-service time, and accessory/gift cost.

## Channel split

- **Xiaohongshu**: scenario proof, comparison notes, setup/use experience, aesthetic/home/lifestyle fit, search mindshare.
- **Douyin short video**: fast demo, problem-solution contrast, unboxing, before/after workflow where compliant, live-room handoff.
- **Douyin self-live**: education, demo, bundle explanation, Q&A, service and warranty reassurance.
- **Talent livestream**: useful only when creator audience, script, price, service promise, and refund economics are locked.
- **Tmall**: flagship trust, detail-page proof, reviews, brand search, member repurchase/accessories.
- **JD**: parameter comparison, official authenticity, fast delivery, invoice/warranty, after-sale confidence.
- **WeChat/private domain**: setup tutorials, warranty registration, accessory/refill reminders, firmware/app education, member service.
- **Pinduoduo/Kuaishou**: use only with isolated value pack, old model, or explicit value-channel logic and profit/price controls.

## Content and keyword directions

Build content around:

- Scenario: small home, office, travel, parenting, pet, outdoor, sleep, cooking, cleaning, commuting, gaming, creator workflow.
- Parameter: battery, noise, power, capacity, material, compatibility, safety certification if verified, app support, warranty.
- Comparison: old vs new, entry vs pro, competitor/spec comparison only when fair and substantiated.
- Trust: unboxing, setup, real use, teardown/inside view if safe, long-use review, negative-point honesty.
- Service: warranty, returns, installation, repair, customer-service response, spare parts.

Xiaohongshu keyword clusters:

- Product + scenario: "smart home", "small apartment", "desk setup", "travel", "pet home", "cleaning", "sleep".
- Model/spec: model name, capacity, noise, battery, material, compatibility.
- Decision: "is it worth it", "comparison", "review", "unboxing", "setup", "after one month".
- Problem: storage, mess, noise, time-saving, safety, convenience, comfort.

## Douyin script directions

Use:

```text
Problem scene -> 3-second demo -> parameter proof -> real-use detail -> service/warranty reassurance -> bundle/price reason -> search/live landing
```

Avoid scripts that overpromise performance, hide limitations, or create unsupported health/safety claims.

## Tmall/JD/search landing

Landing pages must reduce decision uncertainty:

- Title includes model, spec, scenario, and official brand wording.
- Main image clarifies product type and key benefit without exaggeration.
- Detail page includes parameter table, compatibility, package contents, warranty, service, delivery/installation, app/firmware support if relevant, accessory/refill path, and comparison chart.
- Reviews and Q&A cover setup difficulty, delivery, after-sale, noise/performance, compatibility, durability, and gift/accessory expectations.

## Talent livestream rules

Before booking:

1. Calculate channel net profit with commission, pit fee, sample/device cost, gift/accessory, return freight, warranty reserve, and service cost.
2. Lock demo script, service answers, forbidden claims, and price/benefit stack.
3. Confirm inventory by model/spec/color and replacement units.
4. Ensure the landing page can answer parameter and after-sale questions after the live burst.

Do not use talent live to dump the current hero model at a visibly lower public price unless the brand deliberately accepts price-memory damage.

## Compliance and refund risk checklist

Flag:

- Unsupported performance, safety, health, medical, sterilization, energy-saving, or certification claims.
- Incomplete model/spec/package-content wording.
- Compatibility gaps with phones, apps, accessories, home systems, voltage, or consumables.
- Warranty, installation, or return policy ambiguity.
- High return from expectation gap, noise, size, battery life, setup difficulty, or app instability.
- Negative reviews about after-sale, repair, missing accessories, or delivery damage.

## Repurchase and LTV

Design:

- Device -> accessory/refill.
- Entry model -> pro upgrade.
- Device -> warranty/service membership.
- Setup content -> member community.
- Review/Q&A -> search trust asset.

Track cohorts by model, channel, first-use issue, after-sale cost, accessory attachment, and upgrade/refill behavior.

## Default output additions

For consumer electronics/smart-hardware full plans, always add:

1. Model/spec and service matrix.
2. Warranty/after-sale/install/Q&A plan.
3. Parameter and comparison proof plan.
4. Channel price/spec separation.
5. Return/refund and service-cost sensitivity.
6. Accessory/refill/upgrade lifecycle path.
