# Beauty and Personal Care Category Pack

## Load when

Use this pack for beauty, skincare, haircare, body care, oral care, fragrance, personal care, grooming, and related FMCG categories. This is the default category pack for this skill when the user does not name another category.

## Category operating logic

Beauty and personal care is usually not a pure low-price category. The operating system must balance:

- Trust before conversion: claims, proof, reviews, creator credibility, and usage scenario matter.
- Trial before stock-up: new customers often need a low-risk entry SKU, then routine/bundle repurchase.
- Mindshare before price war: Xiaohongshu and Tmall protect value perception; Douyin converts urgency and visible benefit.
- Compliance before script aggression: avoid unsupported efficacy, medical implication, extreme words, and exaggerated before-after claims.
- Routine before single order: long-term profit comes from repurchase, sets, refills, and member lifecycle.

## SKU matrix

| SKU role | Beauty/personal-care example | Job | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Trial SKU | Mini, sample, travel size, first-order pack | Lower first purchase barrier | Long-term low AOV if not connected to repurchase |
| Hero SKU | Core serum, cleanser, shampoo, body lotion, sunscreen | Main GMV and proof point | Easy cross-channel price comparison |
| Routine bundle | Cleanser + serum, shampoo + conditioner, body wash + lotion | Raise AOV and routine lock-in | Complex explanation if benefits are unclear |
| Problem-solution SKU | Oil control, dandruff care, repair, moisturizing, whitening-adjacent safe claim | Match pain-point demand | High claim/compliance risk |
| Premium image SKU | High-end line, limited scent, upgraded ingredient line | Brand mindshare and margin | Needs stronger proof and content |
| Refill/repurchase SKU | Refill pack, large size, multi-pack | LTV and member path | Logistics, leakage, storage, and price comparison |
| Talent-exclusive pack | Hero SKU + exclusive gift/sample | Creator conversion and price separation | Gift cost and channel fairness |
| Campaign stock-up pack | 2/3/4-pack or routine set | 618/Double 11 burst | Inventory and refund pressure |

## Price and margin strategy

Use SKU/spec/benefit separation instead of direct price cuts:

- Tmall: standard SKU, review trust, official flagship price anchor.
- Douyin self-live: standard price plus gift/sample, routine bundle, or limited-time member benefit.
- Douyin talent livestream: exclusive pack, larger bundle, creator gift, or special routine set to avoid direct comparison.
- Xiaohongshu: explain value, ingredient/proof, scenario, and routine; do not lead with lowest price.
- Member/private: refill, repurchase coupon, routine subscription, replenishment reminder.

Protect these lines:

- Brand value price: the price that supports perceived ingredient/proof/routine value.
- Daily transaction price: sustainable daily deal level.
- Minimum profit price: do not let live, creator, or campaign permanently break it.

## Creator and content fit

Creator types:

- Dermatology/science-education style creators where claims can be substantiated and compliant.
- Beauty reviewers for texture, usage, finish, scent, and comparison.
- Routine creators for skincare/haircare/body-care steps.
- Scenario creators for oily scalp, dry skin, commute, gym, summer, travel, gifting.
- KOC and real-user review matrix for trust and long-tail search.
- Value creators for campaign packs only if price line is protected.

Avoid creators whose usual style depends on exaggerated efficacy, medical treatment language, extreme before-after, or aggressive competitor attack.

## Xiaohongshu keyword directions

Build keywords by:

- Brand words: brand name, hero product name, flagship terms.
- Category words: serum, cleanser, shampoo, body lotion, sunscreen, mouthwash, fragrance.
- Benefit words: moisturizing, oil control, repair, refreshing, gentle, long-lasting scent, smooth hair.
- Scenario words: oily scalp in summer, dry skin in air conditioning, post-gym body care, travel mini pack.
- Audience words: sensitive-skin cautious wording, oily-hair users, office workers, students, new moms only if category-compliant.
- Problem words: flaky scalp, dry patches, dull-looking skin, body odor scenario, rough hair.
- Ingredient/material words: only if formula proof exists.
- Competitor words: use cautiously; prefer comparison of scenarios and selection criteria over direct attack.

## Douyin content directions

Use formats with visible proof and fast comprehension:

- First 3 seconds: name the pain/scenario, not the ingredient list.
- Demo: texture, foam, absorption, oil-control scene, hair smoothness, scent scenario, pack value.
- Social proof: reviews, repeat purchase, routine usage, creator experience where substantiated.
- Live handoff: explain why the live pack differs from Tmall standard SKU.
- Product card/search: title and cover must match the pain or routine, not only brand wording.

Script structure:

```text
Pain/scenario -> product role -> proof or demo -> safe benefit language -> pack/price reason -> who should buy -> who should not buy -> live/search landing
```

## Tmall and search landing

Tmall must carry trust:

- Title covers brand, category, benefit, scenario, and hero SKU name.
- Main image communicates product role and pack value without illegal claims.
- Detail page separates ingredient/proof, usage routine, applicable audience, non-applicable audience, reviews, Q&A, and after-sale promise.
- Reviews and buyer shows matter more than aggressive discounts for mid/high-price beauty products.
- Member path should push refill, routine bundle, replenishment cycle, and old-customer-only benefit.

## Talent livestream rules

Talent livestream works when:

- Selling point can be explained in under 30 seconds.
- Pack benefit is clear without breaking Tmall's standard price.
- Creator has trust with the target skin/hair/body-care scenario.
- Refund and complaint risks are controlled.
- Inventory, gifts, and samples are locked before scheduling.

Model the deal before booking:

```text
Talent net profit =
talent GMV
- product cost
- platform fee
- creator commission
- pit fee
- gift/sample cost
- fulfillment cost
- refund loss
- group leader/service fee
- content authorization fee
```

If net profit is negative, label the cooperation as acquisition/seeding budget and define repurchase payback; do not call it a profit channel.

## Compliance risk checklist

Verify current legal/platform rules before final claims. Flag:

- Medical treatment implication.
- Unsupported efficacy or time-bound result.
- Absolute terms such as "best", "No.1", "permanent", "100%".
- Exaggerated before-after comparison.
- Claims around acne, whitening, anti-hair-loss, repair, allergy, baby/pregnancy safety, or health effects without approved evidence.
- Creator scripts that say more than the brand can substantiate.
- Competitor comparison that implies unverifiable superiority.

Safer pattern:

```text
Describe observable texture/scenario/routine experience, cite available proof conservatively, define suitable and unsuitable users, and avoid treatment-like promises.
```

## Repurchase and LTV

Design repurchase paths by usage cycle:

- Trial -> standard size.
- Standard size -> routine bundle.
- Routine bundle -> refill or multi-pack.
- Campaign stock-up -> replenishment reminder.
- Member -> old-customer coupon, scent/variant extension, limited routine set.

Track repurchase by SKU role, not only by total channel GMV. A trial SKU that does not convert to standard or routine bundle is a weak acquisition SKU.

## Default output additions

For beauty/personal-care full plans, always add:

1. Claim/compliance risk section.
2. Routine and repurchase design.
3. Tmall trust/review landing.
4. Xiaohongshu search keyword matrix.
5. Talent-live pack separation from Tmall standard SKU.
6. Refund/complaint watchlist: allergy/discomfort, mismatch of expectation, leakage, gift missing, delayed shipment, scent/texture disagreement.
