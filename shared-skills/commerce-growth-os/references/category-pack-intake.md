# Category Pack Intake

## Load when

Use this reference when the user names a category, asks for category-specific strategy, asks for a premium/mid-to-high-end consumer brand plan, or requests category packs.

## Rule

Do not invent category-specific compliance, legal, platform, or performance benchmarks as facts. Use category playbooks as operating hypotheses unless the user provides brand data or official platform/legal references are verified.

Default category for this skill is beauty and personal care. When the user does not specify a category, read `category-packs/beauty-personal-care.md`.

For premium or mid-to-high-end brands, avoid low-price white-label logic unless the user explicitly asks for low-price distribution. Protect brand value, proof, search trust, review quality, and repurchase path before proposing aggressive discounting or mass traffic scale.

## Category fields to collect

- Category and subcategory.
- Main consumption scenario and decision trigger.
- Purchase frequency and repurchase cycle.
- Shelf life, storage, shipping, breakage/leakage risk.
- Regulatory/compliance boundary.
- Price bands and target AOV.
- Core proof type: ingredient, effect, taste, material, safety, convenience, value, certification, review.
- Main competitors and their price/spec.
- Creator types that users trust.
- Common refund/complaint causes.
- Search terms across Xiaohongshu, Douyin, and Tmall.

## Starter category directions

Beauty and personal care:

- Prioritize claim compliance, ingredient/proof clarity, before-after safety, trial pack, routine bundle, creator credibility, Xiaohongshu search, and Tmall review trust.
- For the full default pack, read `category-packs/beauty-personal-care.md`.

Food and beverage:

- For premium brands, read `category-packs/premium-food-and-beverage.md`.
- Prioritize taste proof, origin/craft, giftability, scenario, repurchase pack, shelf life, logistics, sampling, bundle AOV, and campaign stock-up.

Mother and baby:

- Read `category-packs/premium-mother-and-baby.md`.
- Prioritize safety proof, certification, conservative claims, review trust, expert/parent creator credibility, and customer-service assurance.

Pet:

- Read `category-packs/premium-pet.md`.
- Prioritize pet-owner trust, ingredient/proof clarity, problem-solution scenario, palatability/use proof, subscription/refill, shipping weight, and refund reasons.

Home care and paper:

- For premium home fragrance or lifestyle care, read `category-packs/premium-home-fragrance-and-care.md`.
- For home paper, household daily necessities, cleaning consumables, and value-pack household products, read `category-packs/home-paper-and-daily-necessities.md`.
- Prioritize value pack, scenario demo, stock-up logic, logistics cost, bundle margin, and household repurchase cycle.

Consumer electronics and smart hardware:

- Read `category-packs/consumer-electronics-and-smart-hardware.md`.
- Prioritize parameter clarity, model/spec matrix, service/warranty, JD/Tmall search landing, demo proof, Q&A/reviews, after-sale cost, and channel price/spec separation.

Nutrition and health:

- If the product targets older adults or family health lifestyle, read `category-packs/silver-lifestyle-wellness.md`.
- If the product is nutrition food or functional food, read `category-packs/nutrition-and-functional-food.md`.
- Prioritize compliance, claim boundaries, certification/proof, expert review caution, taste/routine proof, repurchase cycle, and no medical-treatment implication.

Apparel, shoes, and accessories:

- Read `category-packs/premium-apparel-and-accessories.md` for premium/mid-to-high brands.
- Prioritize style/occasion, size and fit proof, return risk, seasonal drop cadence, buyer-show quality, price-line protection, and size/color inventory.

## Category-pack expansion format

When creating a detailed category pack, include:

1. Category economics and price bands.
2. SKU matrix and bundle logic.
3. Channel split.
4. Creator types.
5. Xiaohongshu keywords.
6. Douyin script directions.
7. Tmall keyword matrix.
8. Talent livestream commission logic.
9. Compliance red zones.
10. Repurchase cycle and member path.
11. Campaign rhythm.
12. Service, warranty, logistics, or after-sale risk when category-specific.
