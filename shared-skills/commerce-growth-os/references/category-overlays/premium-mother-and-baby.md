# Premium Mother and Baby Overlay

## Use when

Use for premium maternal, infant, toddler, feeding, care, hygiene, safety, developmental, or family-support products.

## Demand and trust

- The buyer may differ from the user; parents, grandparents, caregivers, and gift buyers have different evidence needs.
- Safety, suitability by age/stage, professional trust, material/ingredient transparency, and service confidence dominate impulse novelty.
- Decisions are shaped by risk avoidance, peer proof, stage transitions, and family routines.

## Product and evidence

- Record age/stage, use environment, supervision needs, material/ingredient, size, compatibility, cleaning, storage, expiry, and warnings.
- Evidence must distinguish regulatory compliance, safety testing, expert guidance, product performance, and user experience.

## Commercial risks

- Stage-limited use can shorten LTV; model migration to the next stage, replenishment, gifting, and sibling/family reuse.
- Samples, education, service, returns, bulky logistics, and trust-building costs can be material.

## Operations and supply risks

- Prioritize batch traceability, recall readiness, packaging integrity, expiry, sterilization or cleaning instructions, size/fit, and replacement parts where relevant.
- Customer service must recognize safety events and escalate without speculative advice.

## Compliance boundaries

- Avoid unsupported medical, developmental, intelligence, immunity, allergy, sleep, or guaranteed-safety claims.
- Clearly distinguish food, supplement, device, toy, care, and general consumer-product rules.

## Lifecycle

- Model pregnancy/age/stage transitions, replenishment intervals, gifting, family decision roles, and churn at stage completion.

## Specialist application

Product and Supply Chain must eventually own safety/specification and traceability; current Commerce and Marketing skills must surface these dependencies rather than invent assurances.
