# Premium Apparel and Accessories Overlay

## Use when

Use for premium apparel, footwear, bags, accessories, sports/outdoor apparel, seasonal collections, or fit-sensitive products.

## Demand and trust

- Demand is driven by style, identity, fit, function, material, occasion, social proof, and confidence in quality.
- Trust depends on accurate fit/size, material and construction detail, real-person representation, care, durability, and return expectations.

## Product and evidence

- Record fit, size system, measurements, model/body references, material, construction, color, care, weather/function limits, and compatibility.
- Use representative try-on, movement, detail, comparison, and use-condition evidence without deceptive retouching.

## Commercial risks

- Size-color depth, seasonality, markdown, returns, reverse logistics, damaged packaging, and variant fragmentation dominate economics.
- Separate acquisition demand from inventory availability; traffic to unavailable variants destroys conversion and brand trust.

## Operations and supply risks

- Monitor size accuracy, color/material variance, defects, labeling, packaging, stock by variant, exchange, return reasons, repair, and seasonal aging.
- Distinguish fit, preference, quality defect, expectation mismatch, and fulfillment error.

## Compliance boundaries

- Functional, weather, protection, sustainability, material-origin, and performance claims require defined test scope and conditions.

## Lifecycle

- Model seasonal drops, wardrobe use, replacement, collection, cross-category attachment, markdown risk, and product-care engagement.

## Specialist application

Commercial owns variant and markdown economics; Content owns fit evidence; Operations owns size/return execution; Analytics must report conversion and returns by size, color, style, and cohort.
