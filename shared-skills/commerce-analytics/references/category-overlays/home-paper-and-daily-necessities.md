# Home Paper and Daily Necessities Overlay

## Use when

Use for tissue, paper goods, household consumables, cleaning, hygiene, commodity-like daily necessities, or bulky repeat-purchase packs.

## Demand and trust

- Demand is driven by value-in-use, quality consistency, household fit, convenience, safety, storage, and replenishment reliability.
- Trust depends on specification clarity, material/ingredient, count/weight, real usage, cleaning performance, and transparent comparison.

## Product and evidence

- Record unit/count/weight, dimensions, layers/concentration, material/ingredient, compatible surfaces/use, dosage, safety, storage, and shelf life.
- Normalize comparisons to cost per use, effective quantity, performance, and logistics rather than headline pack price.

## Commercial risks

- Freight, bulky packaging, breakage/leakage, subsidy, low AOV, channel price comparison, refund, and promotion dependence dominate economics.
- Bulk packs can improve unit logistics but create storage, damage, and repeat-cycle distortion.

## Operations and supply risks

- Monitor specification consistency, packaging compression, leakage, damage, contamination, count variance, warehouse space, delivery, and complaint patterns.
- Distinguish product performance, misuse, surface incompatibility, packaging failure, and logistics damage.

## Compliance boundaries

- Antibacterial, disinfection, safety, child/pet, environmental, material, flushability, and performance claims require defined evidence and conditions.

## Lifecycle

- Model household size, usage rate, replenishment, subscription, pack size, storage limits, seasonality, and channel-exclusive specifications.

## Specialist application

Commercial must include freight and channel price risk; Growth must avoid subsidy-dependent scale; Operations must protect pack/logistics quality; Analytics must use normalized units and household cohorts.
