# Premium Food and Beverage Overlay

## Use when

Use for premium packaged food, beverage, snacks, gifting, origin/craft products, seasonal food, or taste-led consumption.

## Demand and trust

- Demand may be personal enjoyment, health-conscious choice, social sharing, gifting, ritual, convenience, or origin/craft appreciation.
- Trust depends on taste expectation, ingredient/origin transparency, freshness, production credibility, packaging, and delivery condition.

## Product and evidence

- Record flavor, texture, serving occasion, ingredient, allergen, nutrition, origin, process, storage, expiry, serving size, and preparation.
- Separate regulated labels, certificates, origin/process evidence, sensory tests, and consumer preference.

## Commercial risks

- Gift packaging, samples, breakage, cold chain, shipping weight, expiry, seasonality, and promotion can materially reduce margin.
- Gift-box AOV can hide weak repeat purchase; separate gifting cohorts from self-consumption cohorts.

## Operations and supply risks

- Monitor freshness, lot/expiry, temperature, seal, leakage, breakage, taste variance, inventory aging, recall readiness, and peak-season capacity.
- Refund diagnosis should distinguish logistics damage, quality defect, taste preference, expectation mismatch, and storage failure.

## Compliance boundaries

- Avoid unsupported health, disease, functional, weight, or guaranteed-origin claims.
- Make allergens, nutrition, storage, alcohol/caffeine, age restrictions, and preparation requirements explicit where applicable.

## Lifecycle

- Model personal replenishment, gifting calendar, holidays, seasonality, flavor rotation, subscription, and pantry consumption separately.

## Specialist application

Commercial Strategy must include logistics and expiry loss; Marketing must separate gifting and self-use demand; Operations must protect freshness and peak fulfillment; Analytics must separate cohorts and spoilage/refund causes.
