# Premium Pet Overlay

## Use when

Use for premium pet food, supplements, hygiene, grooming, care, accessories, or pet-lifestyle products.

## Demand and trust

- The purchaser interprets outcomes for an animal that cannot report directly; trust depends on transparency, palatability, tolerance, professional or credible evidence, and observed routine outcomes.
- Segment by species, breed/size, age, condition, environment, feeding/grooming routine, and owner lifestyle.

## Product and evidence

- Record ingredient/material, nutrition or functional role, dosage/use, transition method, storage, expiry, compatibility, and contraindications.
- Separate guaranteed analysis or specification, professional guidance, product tests, owner observation, and anecdotal experience.

## Commercial risks

- Trial rejection, palatability, transition periods, shipping weight, samples, subscription discounts, and refund policy can change contribution.
- LTV depends on actual consumption cycle, household pet count, adherence, and trust retention.

## Operations and supply risks

- Monitor batch consistency, contamination, packaging seal, odor/texture variance, temperature, expiry, traceability, and complaint escalation.
- Distinguish product defect, transition reaction, misuse, preference rejection, and unrelated health events.

## Compliance boundaries

- Avoid unsupported treatment, cure, prevention, guaranteed health, or universal suitability claims.
- Health-sensitive cases require veterinary or qualified professional referral rather than speculative advice.

## Lifecycle

- Model trial, transition, routine adoption, replenishment/subscription, age-stage change, and churn from palatability or trust events.

## Specialist application

Content must translate evidence conservatively; Growth must protect subscription quality; Operations must support traceability and sensitive complaints; Analytics must segment by pet and consumption cycle.
