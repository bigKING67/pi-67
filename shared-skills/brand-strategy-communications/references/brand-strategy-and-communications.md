# Brand Strategy and Communications

## 1. Brand platform

Define the smallest coherent brand platform:

- Category frame: the market and alternatives the brand competes against.
- Target audience: priority user, buyer, influencer, and excluded audience.
- Consumer tension: the unresolved functional, emotional, or social problem.
- Brand promise: the durable value the brand commits to create.
- Reasons to believe: product, proof, authority, experience, and behavior.
- Personality and tone: how the brand behaves consistently.
- Distinctive assets: name, symbols, colors, shapes, phrases, sounds, characters, and rituals.
- Message house: one core message with audience- and channel-specific support.

Do not change positioning merely because a channel or campaign needs novelty. Separate durable brand choices from temporary campaign expressions.

## 2. Integrated communications

Build communications from objective to mechanism:

1. Business objective.
2. Brand or reputation objective.
3. Audience and desired belief or behavior change.
4. Core narrative and proof.
5. Communication mechanism: campaign, PR, spokesperson, sponsorship, partnership, event, or crisis response.
6. Owned, earned, paid, shared, offline, and commerce activation.
7. Asset plan and reuse rights.
8. Measurement, approvals, risks, and exit gates.

Brand awareness is not a sufficient objective. Specify salience, association, trust, consideration, preference, search, trial, advocacy, or reputation outcome.

## 3. PR and media

Use PR when credible third-party attention, category education, issue leadership, executive authority, or reputation matters.

Define:

- Newsworthy fact or point of view.
- Evidence and source owner.
- Priority media and stakeholder tiers.
- Spokesperson and approval chain.
- Embargo, release, response, and correction process.
- Expected earned outcomes and limitations.

Do not invent facts, endorsements, tests, certifications, or media interest.

## 4. Spokesperson and ambassador

Distinguish roles:

- Spokesperson: broad, formal, durable brand representation.
- Ambassador: sustained association, often narrower than full spokesperson rights.
- Friend of brand: flexible, lighter, campaign or event association.
- Campaign partner: time-bounded activation around one idea or launch.
- Expert or cultural partner: authority or meaning in a specific domain.
- Creator/KOL: content and community influence; usually owned by Content/Social.

Evaluate candidates across:

- Brand, audience, category, cultural, and channel fit.
- Awareness, affinity, credibility, distinctiveness, and overexposure.
- Existing endorsements, category conflicts, exclusivity, and future option value.
- Reputation, conduct, legal, political, health, fraud, and platform risks.
- Rights: name, likeness, voice, content, derivatives, edits, paid media, commerce, offline, territory, channels, and term.
- Activation: announcement, brand film, content, product, retail, live, event, PR, employee, and membership use.
- Measurement: brand lift, search, earned media, content quality, conversion contribution, asset reuse, and long-term association.
- Exit: suspension, takedown, replacement, crisis response, refund, and asset retirement.

Never judge a spokesperson only by follower count or recent popularity.

## 5. Sponsorship and co-branding

Require a strategic link between the partner property and the desired brand association. Evaluate audience overlap, attention quality, distinctive access, rights, activation cost, channel leverage, product fit, reputational risk, and exit conditions.

The rights fee is not the full cost. Include activation, content, media, retail, hospitality, production, legal, and opportunity cost.

## 6. Crisis communications

Classify severity by consumer harm, factual uncertainty, legal/regulatory risk, spread velocity, stakeholder scope, and business continuity.

Use this sequence:

1. Verify facts and preserve evidence.
2. Name the incident owner and approval chain.
3. Stop harmful or misleading activity.
4. Communicate what is known, unknown, and being done.
5. Avoid speculation and contradictory channel responses.
6. Provide remedy, update cadence, and completion criteria.
7. Review root cause and reputation recovery.

Brand tone never overrides safety, truth, or legal obligations.
