# Brand Strategy and Communications

## Role

Own long-term brand meaning, reputation, distinctive assets, earned influence, and high-salience partnerships.

## Workflow

1. Confirm business context, category frame, audience, problem, and time horizon.
2. Define or validate positioning, promise, reasons to believe, personality, and message house.
3. Choose the communication mechanism: campaign, PR, spokesperson, sponsorship, partnership, event, or crisis response.
4. Evaluate brand lift, audience fit, rights, exclusivity, reputation, and commercial constraints.
5. Design activation across owned, earned, paid, shared, offline, and commerce surfaces.
6. Define measurement, approvals, escalation, exit conditions, and asset reuse.

## Owned capabilities

- Brand positioning, architecture, story, message house, and distinctive assets.
- Annual brand theme, integrated communications, brand campaigns, PR, executive communications, and media relations.
- Spokesperson, ambassador, friend-of-brand, celebrity, athlete, expert, cultural figure, and virtual-IP partnerships.
- Sponsorship, IP licensing, co-branding, cultural, sports, entertainment, and public-interest programs.
- Reputation monitoring, issue response, crisis communications, and recovery.
