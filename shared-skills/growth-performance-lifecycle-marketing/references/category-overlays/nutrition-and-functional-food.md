# Nutrition and Functional Food Overlay

## Use when

Use for nutrition food, functional snacks, protein, low-sugar products, wellness beverages, meal support, or subscription-oriented consumables.

## Demand and trust

- Demand may be convenience, taste, dietary preference, sports, weight-management support, energy, routine, or perceived wellness.
- Trust depends on ingredient, nutrition, dosage/serving, taste, adherence, evidence, and realistic expectations.

## Product and evidence

- Record ingredient, nutrition facts, allergen, serving, frequency, target audience, contraindications, storage, expiry, taste, texture, and preparation.
- Separate regulated nutrition labels, authorized claims, product tests, expert explanation, and consumer experience.

## Commercial risks

- Sampling, flavor rejection, shipping weight, subscription discount, expiry, refund, and retention incentives can erase contribution.
- Subscription or repeat forecasts must use adherence and actual consumption, not theoretical serving count alone.

## Operations and supply risks

- Monitor ingredient/lot consistency, contamination, allergen control, seal, taste variance, temperature, expiry, traceability, and recall readiness.
- Complaint handling must distinguish preference, intolerance, misuse, defect, and unrelated health events.

## Compliance boundaries

- Avoid disease, treatment, guaranteed weight, immunity, detox, hormonal, metabolic, or universal health claims unless specifically lawful and evidenced.
- Clearly distinguish ordinary food, special food, supplement, sports nutrition, and medical-use categories.

## Lifecycle

- Model trial, taste acceptance, routine adoption, adherence, replenishment, subscription, flavor rotation, and churn.

## Specialist application

Product and Supply Chain must own formula, allergen, quality, and traceability; Marketing must translate evidence conservatively; Analytics must measure real adherence and cohort retention.
