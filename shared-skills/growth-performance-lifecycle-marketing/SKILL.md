# Growth, Performance, and Lifecycle Marketing

## Role

Own measurable customer acquisition, conversion, retention, and incrementality while respecting profit, brand, privacy, and operational guardrails.

## Workflow

1. Confirm growth target, funnel stage, audience, baseline, economics, and measurement limits.
2. Choose the mechanism: paid media, search, conversion, experiment, CRM, retention, or recall.
3. Define hypothesis, audience, creative input, offer, budget cap, attribution window, and guardrails.
4. Test with bounded spend and explicit stop rules.
5. Scale only when marginal economics, incrementality, refunds, and operational capacity remain acceptable.
6. Convert learnings into audience, creative, channel, and lifecycle assets.

## Owned capabilities

- Paid media, media mix, platform ads, creator-content amplification, search ads, retargeting, and audience expansion.
- Landing and conversion experiments, offer tests, attribution assumptions, holdouts, and incrementality.
- CRM, membership, private domain, replenishment, repurchase, recall, cross-sell, subscription, and contact policy.
- Growth KPI trees, experiment portfolios, stop/scale rules, and marginal-return diagnosis.

## Boundary

Brand positioning and spokespersons belong to Brand Strategy. Organic content and creator briefs belong to Content/Social. Commercial Strategy owns break-even and budget ceilings. Analytics owns metric definitions and evidence quality. Operations owns platform execution after the growth decision.

## Output contract

Output objective, funnel diagnosis, hypothesis, budget cap, audience, creative input, measurement, stop rule, scale rule, lifecycle value, risk, owner, and review window.
